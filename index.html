About myself

Hey there! I'm Abdulaziz, born and raised in Sana'a, Yemen. Picture this: a bustling household with 7 older siblings (yep, I'm the baby of the family), all now married with kids of their own. Growing up in Yemen shaped a big part of who I am today - the friendships, the culture, it's all part of my DNA.

Life took an unexpected turn when the war started in 2015, and I found myself packing my bags for the US. Talk about a change of scenery! I dove headfirst into college life, choosing Software Engineering as my field (because who doesn't love solving puzzles?). It was during these college years that I met my ride-or-die friends - the ones who've seen me through late-night coding sessions and ultimate frisbee tournaments alike.

Fast forward to now, and you'll find me in Seattle, crafting code for Amazon. It's a far cry from the streets of Sana'a, but hey, life's an adventure, right? Through it all, my Yemeni roots and my love for both cultures continue to shape my worldview and my dreams for the future.

How do you resolve a conflict?

I normally like to assess what is the cause of a conflict before resolving it. Is it a temporary heat caused by a stressful situation? Is it caused by miscommunication? Fear? Expectation? .. etc. If the issue is not a life changing decision, then there are many ways to deal with it, not going to list them in this doc. However, if the issue is a life changing situation then I do the following:

  • Sit and talk logically about it respecting the values that we both value. What does religion say about it? If it's not religion related, what are the pros and cons? If that doesn't help, can I compromise with no harm? .. etc
  • If the above doesn't work, then a third eye might help. I normally pick a person that we both trust, and hope they bring a fresh idea, or help us see the correct choice.
